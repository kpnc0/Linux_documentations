This is a surface level analysis of the AI tech boom and the economy, and alternative cheaper ways to access intelligence.

## The state of the silicon valley.
> Companies are hyping AI. They just want more investor money. Common free users don't matter. 

> Common users are a liability until they start paying up.

> Companies are incentivised to provide free usage to common users so they some day convert to paying loyal users.

Now, the reason why the tokens are even provided for free for common users is for the above reason.

And now this brings us to the part the investors play in the funnel:

> Their money enables the AI companies to provide free or subsidized use of their AI models. And their money funds their new models and hardware.

So, tweets and the hype are all strategic. To increase their user base and make the free users pay.

## But, they are running out of investor money.

*you* may have heard of all the money raised by openAI during round x. They seem like daily news, but these are the things that kept most the things cheap.

However, I theorize that all of this might end as investors demand profitability.

If the existing investors stop paying to keep the company afloat, they would see their past investment go worthless. This is the concorde effect, but honestly, I don't see investors pulling out, because they kinda seem to know the long term goals. ?

## Time to make them pay.

Now, the AI companies want the common folks to pay up. They are incentivised to do it. Either that, or literally reduce the spending on the common users.

The latter is a more.. sustainable option. 

So, grok system messages common users about their servers being in high demand,
and google is ending google gemini-cli for **normal users** (including PRO plan).

And for paying people, the API costs saw a rise in price, or the quantity (not quality) of service detiriorate.

## What this means for common folks.

Prepare to be treated as parasites.

You don't **matter** if you don't become a paying user. But you are tollerated in hopes that maybe you will pay.. someday, and better soon.

One would have to pivot to other providers. 

## This year will be the year of "reoptimization"

AI companies, as I said **before**, will optimize for maximizing revenue, minimizing loss, maximizing future revenue, competitor elimination.

Do not be surprised when the free tier will totally disappear from the major companies.

## The possible end of the bubble:

The AI flywheel is one part of the story. In the united states, data centers have a high cost of setting up, but are generating revenue, possibly hiting even in {to be computed later} years. And this is even in China, and India and Europe.

If in case AI companies pull out of the data centers, the revenue of the data center suffers. Which is obvious.

NVIDIA provides hardware for the AI companies. Global supply chain for computing hardware seems to be incentivised to pivot to supplying AI companies. And due to only a few influential companies having monopoly on hardware production, it is relatively easy for them to keep the consumer prices high.. by forming a **cartel**

Who suffers due to an AI company bankruptcy: data centers, supply chains, investors.

Who suffers the worst: **Investors**

On further thought, I seem to fail to find a vector from which this bubble can burst. Dot com bubble involved a frenzy where new startups went public for big money (due to trust that it will perform). But this specific bubble, the **AI bubble**, is more focused on technological growth and research. 

Modes where the AI companies profits:
* Companies contracting them to provide tokens,
* Paying users.
* Defence contracts.

Money comes from providing actual value.

Point of failure:
* Outcompeted by breakthrough models.
* End of investor money (I must use this to recompute the possibility of the bubble burst) (as I discussed before, investors are incentivised to keep pouring money into the companies)
