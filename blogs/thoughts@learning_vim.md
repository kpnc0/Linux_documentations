## So, it started with me switching to void linux (musl)

After installing **void linux** in my hardware, I experienced, perhaps for the first time, the freedom of using a general purpose hardware. It was **liberating** without the needless bloat.

But a problem arose: flatpak app installation was a massive download (downloading gilbc runtime environments), meaning I couldn't install **sublime text editor**.

I initially started editing texts in void linux in mousepad. Then when querying deepseek, it recommended me to use `vi <your file name>` command for text editing.

## Enter neovim

Initially, when I used it, it was terrible. vi felt like a bitch. So, I installed `neovim`. And even that felt terrible to use, but more tollerable (because backspace worked).

Somehow, I was convinced to use vim. I learnt basic modes like `i` and `Esc`. Then `dd` to delete entire lines, et al.

And now, I am a bit more comfortable with neovim.

Approximately a few hours of total `neovim` use, combined with `neovim` being the default editor, and proper sleep, I began to get more effective with it.

Even community fourums were filled with senior engineers telling us about how they **used** vim for coding. I actually didn't think one could use that for programming. I was proven wrong. I **believed** that sublime was minimalistic compared to VScode, but `vim` is less bloated, and effecient, like a different motor organ.

## Future scope

I just need more patience and practice. Right now, vim is starting to feel more faster than using mousepad. Perhaps the **theory** was right.

The only bottleneck now will be my coding competence and reasoning.

I still have a long way to go, and more to learn.
